    About the Mission
    Waste to Wealth is Fularani Foundation's circular economy initiative — developed in partnership with Gnufox Pvt. Ltd. and Bhadrak Municipality. The programme tackles the twin crises of municipal solid waste management and household income generation by creating systems that turn waste into value.

  
  
    Swachh Bhadrak Sankalp 2025 — Waste segregation guidelines launch with Bhadrak Municipality & Gnufox Pvt. Ltd.
  




    At its core, Waste to Wealth operates an e-Wallet incentive model: households that segregate and deposit dry and organic waste receive digital credits redeemable for essential goods, utility payments, or cash — making responsible waste disposal financially rewarding rather than burdensome.
    Waste is not a problem — it is misplaced value. Waste to Wealth builds the infrastructure to reclaim that value and return it to the communities that generate it.
    Programme Architecture
    
      📱e-Wallet PlatformDigital wallet system awarding credits per kg of segregated waste deposited — redeemable for goods or transferred to bank accounts.
      🏭Processing InfrastructureGnufox technology converts organic waste to compost and dry waste to recycled raw materials — reducing landfill volume.
      🏛️Municipal PartnershipFormal MOU with Bhadrak Municipality for waste collection integration — aligning with SBM 2.0 goals.
      👩‍👩‍👧SHG EmploymentWomen's self-help groups employed as waste aggregators and sorters — creating sustainable livelihoods within the system.
      🌱Compost DistributionOrganic waste converted to vermicompost distributed to Krishi-Sakha farmers and Mission Green plantation sites.
      📊Impact DashboardReal-time waste diversion metrics, e-wallet transaction data and employment tracking — available to municipal and donor partners.
    
    Linkages Across the Foundation Ecosystem
    Waste to Wealth is deeply integrated with other Foundation missions: compost from organic waste feeds Mission Green plantation sites and Krishi-Sakha farmers; e-wallet income supports Mission Sahara beneficiaries; SHG employment connects to Mission Period Pride women's networks. This ecosystem approach maximises impact per rupee invested.
    CSR Opportunity
    Corporate CSR investment can fund e-wallet platform development, processing equipment, SHG training, and municipal integration for one ward or block. A ₹10–15 lakh grant can establish a fully operational Waste to Wealth hub serving 500+ households, processing 2 tonnes/day, and employing 15–20 SHG members — with a clear path to revenue self-sufficiency within 18 months.
