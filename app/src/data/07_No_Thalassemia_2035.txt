    About the Mission
    No Thalassemia Odisha 2035 is Fularani Foundation's most ambitious public health programme — a district-to-state campaign to eliminate new thalassemia births in Odisha within a decade. Thalassemia Major is a genetic blood disorder requiring life-long blood transfusions every 21 days. It is entirely preventable through carrier screening before marriage or conception.

  
    
    
  
  




    Despite being one of India's most thalassemia-affected states, Odisha has no systematic carrier screening programme. Mission No Thalassemia 2035 aims to change that — starting from Bhadrak district with official patronage from the District Administration and scaling to all 30 districts by 2035.
    Thalassemia Major is 100% preventable. Every child born with the disease after 2025 represents a screening system that failed. Our mission is to build that system — in Bhadrak first, then Odisha.
    Mission Components
    
      🧬Carrier Screening CampsFree thalassemia carrier screening camps for youth, pre-marital couples and school students — starting with Bhadrak district.
      📢Mass AwarenessDistrict-wide awareness campaigns through schools, colleges, health fairs, social media and community events.
      🏥DHH IntegrationWorking with DHH Bhadrak to integrate thalassemia screening into existing OPD, antenatal care, and school health protocols.
      📋Patient RegistryBuilding Bhadrak's first comprehensive thalassemia patient registry — enabling data-driven care, transfusion planning and research.
      🏛️Policy AdvocacyEngagement with state government and NHM Odisha to mandate pre-marital thalassemia screening across all districts.
      🌐Thalassemia LighthouseModel treatment ward at DHH Bhadrak providing safe transfusion space, data infrastructure and patient dignity.
    
    April 10, 2026 — District Campaign Launch
    On April 10, 2026, Fularani Foundation is hosting a district-level Thalassemia Awareness Campaign and Patient Gathering at DHH Bhadrak — under official patronage of Shri Dilip Routrai IAS, Collector & DM, Bhadrak. The event features patient testimonials, screening demonstrations, an inter-college awareness competition, and media outreach — marking the formal public launch of Mission No Thalassemia Odisha 2035 in Bhadrak.
    Policy Context
    
      Odisha has one of India's highest thalassemia carrier prevalence rates (3–4% in tribal populations)
      No statewide carrier screening programme currently exists in Odisha
      National Thalassemia Control Programme exists but has low penetration in Odisha
      Fularani Foundation is seeking state government partnership for a district-level pilot
      Target: Zero new thalassemia major births in Bhadrak by 2030; statewide by 2035
