    About the Programme
    Krishi-Sakha AI is a technology-led agricultural innovation developed by Fularani Foundation in collaboration with MITS (Devraj Mohapatra) — an AI-powered crop diagnostics and advisory platform designed for smallholder farmers across Odisha. The platform enables farmers to photograph their crops and receive instant, accurate diagnosis of diseases, pest infestations, and nutrient deficiencies — along with actionable treatment and prevention advice in Odia and Hindi.

  
  
    Fularani Foundation at Veer Surendra Sai University of Technology — Krishi-Sakha AI demonstration & outreach
  



  
  

    Aligned with Odisha's AI Policy 2025, Krishi-Sakha is positioned as a state government implementation partner for agricultural AI — targeting the 4.5 million smallholder farming households in Odisha that currently lack access to timely agronomic advice.
    An Odisha farmer loses 20–40% of their crop to preventable disease every year — simply because no agronomist is available within 50 km. Krishi-Sakha brings the agronomist to every phone.
    Platform Features
    
      📸Visual Crop DiagnosisFarmer photographs affected crop area; AI identifies disease, pest or deficiency with 90%+ accuracy within seconds.
      💊Treatment AdvisoryActionable, affordable treatment recommendations — including organic alternatives and locally available inputs.
      🌾Crop CalendarSeason-specific advisory for Odisha's major crops: paddy, vegetable, pulse and oilseed — aligned with local agro-climatic zones.
      🗣️Multilingual InterfaceFull functionality in Odia, Hindi and English — with voice input support for low-literacy users.
      📡Low-Bandwidth OptimisedDesigned for 2G/3G connectivity in rural areas — lightweight, fast and functional offline for core features.
      🏛️Policy AlignmentAligned with Odisha AI Policy 2025 and positioned for integration with ATMA, KVK and state agriculture dept. systems.
    
    Development Partnership
    Krishi-Sakha is a joint development between Fularani Foundation and MITS (Devraj Mohapatra), combining the Foundation's community access and ground-level Odisha network with MITS's technical AI development capability. The programme is in active development with field trials planned in Bhadrak district.
    Integration with Mission Green & Waste to Wealth
    Krishi-Sakha creates an integrated agroecological loop: organic waste from Waste to Wealth becomes compost distributed through Krishi-Sakha advisory networks; Mission Green plantation sites use Krishi-Sakha for site-species matching; farmers engaged through Krishi-Sakha become Mission Green community stewards — creating a virtuous environmental-agricultural cycle.
